PT Transportasi Gas Indonesia (TGI), hereinafter referred to as “TGI” was established in 2002 as a joint venture between PT Perusahaan Gas Negara Tbk, Transasia Pipeline Pvt. Ltd (“Transasia”) and the PGN Employee Welfare and Retirement Foundation (“YKPP PGN”) with a proportion of shares of 59.87%, 40% and 0.13%, respectively.

As the owner and operator of natural gas pipelines in Indonesia with a total pipeline network of more than 1,000 km, TGI runs a business with a focus on natural gas transportation for the domestic market, namely Sumatra and Batam, as well as for international markets, such as Singapore.

For the sake of consistency in providing the best values, TGI establishes a business philosophy \"Connecting Energy for Synergy\". The word \"Connecting\" represents the good relations that are created and maintained by the existence of gas pipeline transportation as well as in interpersonal relationships. The word \"Energy\" represents natural resources, mental and spiritual strength. While the word \"Synergy\" represents TGI's belief as an entity to continue to grow in a sustainable manner through positive synergies.

TGI has proven a strong reputation through reliable services in the business journey for 2 decades. TGI's experience is supported by human resource assets with world-class capabilities and professionalism, which drive TGI to improve its performance from year to year. TGI always upholds the principle of prudence and responsibility for environmental preservation. This principle is a strong foundation that supports TGI's steps in continuing to provide reliable and trusted services.

Qualified HR quality is TGI's key in realizing its big commitment to operate without stopping (zero-downtime). TGI's success in repairing the 28-inch offshore pipeline without stopping gas supply to Singapore is proof of TGI's reliability and quality of work in providing the best service. This success is also proof of TGI's high commitment to stakeholders, namely Indonesia and Singapore, which are highly dependent on gas pipelines as the main source of gas supply.
